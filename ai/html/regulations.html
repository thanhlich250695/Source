Regulations
Rules
  • Pre-trained models are allowed in the competition (see Guidelines below).
  • We ask that you respect the spirit of the competition and do not cheat. Hand-labeling is forbidden.

ACCOUNT & TEAM

One account per participant

You can participate in the competition as individual or as a team. However, each individual or team can only register as 1 account. You are not allowed to sign up to Zalo AI Challenge from multiple accounts and therefore forbidden to submit from multiple accounts.

No private sharing outside teams

Privately sharing code or data outside of teams is not permitted. It's okay to share code if made available to all participants on the forums.

Team Mergers

Team mergers are allowed and can be performed by the team leader. In order to merge, the combined team must have a total submission count less than or equal to the maximum allowed as of the merge date. The maximum allowed is the number of submissions per day multiplied by the number of days the competition has been running.

Team Limits

There is no maximum team size.

COMPETITION TIMELINE

  • Aug. 08, 2018: Competition Start. Development kit, data (training data, public test data) and evaluation software are made available.
  • Sep. 01, 2018, 11:59PM GMT+7: Deadline for all teams submit final solution including: training code, testing code and models.
  • Sep. 02, 2018, 9AM GMT+7: Private test set released.
  • Sep. 05, 2018, 5pm GMT+7: Final Submission deadline for private test set.
  • Sep. 09, 2018: Challenge results announced.
  • Sept. 15, 2018: Winner(s) presents at Zalo AI Challenge submit

DATA & EVALUATIONS

Guidelines

Participants are restricted to train their algorithms on Zalo AI Challenge 2018 train and validation sets. Pretrained models may be used to construct the algorithms (e.g. ImageNet pretrained models) as long as participants do not actively collect additional data for the target categories of the Zalo AI Challenge 2018 competition. Please specify any and all external data used for training when uploading results.

The general rule is that we want participants to use only the provided training and validation data to train a model to classify the test data. We do not want participants crawling the web in search of additional data for the target categories. Participants should be in the mindset that this is the only data available for those categories.

Participants are allowed to collect additional annotations (e.g. bounding boxes) on the provided training and validation sets. Teams should specify that they collected additional annotations when submitting results.

Submission Limits

You may submit a maximum of 2 entries per day.

You can only select 1 final submissions for judging.

Data and Evaluations

Data of the challenge will be divided into 3 parts: training data, public test data (approximately 49% of test data) and private test data (approximately 51% of test data).

All participants will be able compete on a public test data. Results will be updated on public leaderboard. However, the final results will be based on the other 51% private test data. Before we releasing the private test data for final submission, teams will be asked to freeze model and submit final solution: training and testing code and document briefly describe approach and how to run the code.

Final Submissions and solutions should be uploaded through the challenge server. Noted that there will be time restriction for the final solutions. The final solution of each participant should not run over 6hours on test servers (test server spec will be announced before the competition start)
